Formula Logic

Transparent formulas for investor confidence.

Monthly Revenue = Fee × Subscriptions
Variable Expense = Revenue × (Dev&Infra % + Marketing&Support % + Loss Reserve %)
Profit = Revenue − Variable Expense − Fixed Monthly Cost
Contribution / Subscriber = Fee × (1 − Total Variable %)
Break-even Subscribers = Fixed Cost ÷ Contribution per Subscriber
Customer Lifetime = 1 ÷ Monthly Churn (capped 120 mo)
LTV = Fee × Gross Margin × Lifetime  |  Payback = CAC ÷ (Fee × Gross Margin)
Valuation = (ARR × ARR Mult × ARR Wt) + (Annual Profit × Profit Mult × Profit Wt)

* Glossary of Terms & Abbreviations

Plain-language definitions of the financial and SaaS terms used throughout this dashboard.

Unit Economics & Revenue
ARR — Annual Recurring Revenue: yearly value of recurring subscriptions (monthly recurring revenue × 12).
MRR — Monthly Recurring Revenue: subscription revenue earned in a month (fee × subscribers).
ARPU — Average Revenue Per User: average monthly revenue earned per subscriber.
LTV — Lifetime Value: total gross-margin revenue a customer generates over their lifetime.
CAC — Customer Acquisition Cost: average spend to acquire one paying customer.
LTV:CAC — Ratio of lifetime value to acquisition cost. ≥ 3× is healthy; < 1× loses money per customer.
CAC Payback — Months of gross margin needed to recover the CAC of a customer (≤ 12 months is good for SMB SaaS).
Churn — Percentage of subscribers who cancel each month.
Gross Margin — Share of revenue left after variable costs (dev/infra + marketing/support).
Contribution / Subscriber — Fee remaining per subscriber after variable cost %; funds fixed costs and profit.
Break-even Subscribers — Subscribers required for revenue to cover all costs (zero profit/loss).
Valuation & Investment
MOIC — Multiple On Invested Capital: how many times an investor's money grows (exit value ÷ investment).
IRR — Internal Rate of Return: the annualised (per-year) return rate on an investment.
EMI — Equated Monthly Installment: fixed monthly repayment on a reducing-balance loan.
ARR Multiple — Valuation expressed as a multiple of ARR (e.g. 4× ARR).
Pre-money / Post-money — Company valuation before / after a fundraise (post-money = pre-money + amount raised).
Dilution — Percentage of ownership given to new investors in a round.
Runway — Number of months the business can operate on the cash it holds.
Peak Funding Need — Deepest cumulative cash burned before the business turns cash-positive — the capital to budget/raise.
p.a. — Per annum (per year).
General & Industry
KPI — Key Performance Indicator: a headline metric used to track business health.
SaaS — Software as a Service: software sold on a recurring subscription.
SMB — Small & Medium Business.
MSME — Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises.
MIS — Management Information System: reports/data used for business decisions.
GST — Goods & Services Tax (India).
CA / CS — Chartered Accountant / Company Secretary.
